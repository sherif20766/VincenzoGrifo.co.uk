SPORTS

Football, or I’d better say its street-like version futsal, hasn’t been just a game for me, but an essential part of my life. Can’t live without kicking a ball from time to time. Football is fair and egalitarian: you don’t meet any special-fancy-pricey equipment and a bunch of friends. That’s what attracts me to it. Having played football in a team at a competitive level since an early age has taught me the importance of trust, active listening, collaboration and co-ordination with your teammates to achieve greatest results and be the team at the top of the league. On the top of that, being the goalkeeper, as well as the captain of the team, has nurtured my leadership skills and enhanced my poise and patience by training me to remain fearless, respond decisively and take accountability.

TRAVEL

Travelling is the passion that has shaped my life in the last couple of years. I moved to the UK from Sicily over 3 years ago to learn English and have my first international experience. This was definitely the best decision I ever made, as breaking out my comfort zone and immersing myself into a different culture, society and language, unveiled my weaknesses and strengths making me self-aware. Particularly, living and travelling abroad enhanced my flexibility, perseverance and communication skills. I also learnt the importance of adapting my approach and using multiple perspectives with regards to different social and cultural environments, building up the foundation for a lateral thinking. Travelling made me realise that the room for improvement is infinite and motivates me to broaden my international outlook to keep pushing myself beyond my limits, thus developing both personally and professionally. Mastering English language in only 3 months and backpacking in China for 3 weeks without knowing Mandarin are what I consider my biggest achievements!
